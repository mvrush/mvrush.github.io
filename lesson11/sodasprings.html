About Geyser Song Day

Our annual Geyser Song Day held in February is a Soda Springs tradition you don't want to miss. The Soda Springs geyser is legendary and it's beauty has spawned many songs over the years. Writing a song about the geyser has become so popular that we started Geyser Song Day to accomodate everyone that writes a song.

Every year the town gathers by the geyser and as the geyser erupts, performers play and sing their songs. The songs can only last for as long as the geyser erupts. Since the Soda Springs geyser is controlled by man and not mother nature, if your song is unworthy, the judges stop the geyser and your performance is over.

Geyser Song Day has become so popular that even kings and queens in Europe have travelled the globe to hear the amazing artistry of Geyser Song Day songs. The most notable royal that has attended Geyser Song Day is King Rudolph of Liechtenstein.

Over the years there have been many notable songs written. A greatest hits album was released to rave reviews from Soda Springs locals. Some of the more popular geyser songs that you will hear schoolchildren sing on the streets of Soda Springs have been:

  • The Geyser Washed My Baby Away
  • My Mama Threw Me In The Geyser
  • Geyser Stole My Soul
  • Devil Controls the Geyser
Join us this year for more fabulous songs about the Soda Springs Geyser!
